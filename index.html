Forecasted Time to reach Confidence and Power

Forecasts use latest observed conversion rates and actual projected control/experiment reach growth from pasted cumulative trend data.
Trend required
These estimates are based on current observed control and experiment reach-growth and conversion-rate data. Increases in campaign spend that lead to faster incremental unique reach will change the forecasts; spend that mostly increases frequency or reaches the same users may not shorten the ETA. Changes in conversion rates or conversion lag will also change confidence timing and power requirements.
How to interpret confidence, power, reach, and conversion rates
Confidence vs power in plain English

This distinction matters a lot.

Power is about test size. It asks: "Is the test big enough to reliably detect an effect this large?"

Confidence is about the current result direction. It asks: "Is the observed difference strong enough that the p-value clears the selected confidence threshold?"

SituationMeaning
High confidence, low powerThe result looks significant, but it may be fragile.
High power, low confidenceThe test is big enough, but the result is not convincing.
Low confidence, low powerToo early / too noisy.
High confidence, high powerStrongest decision case.

The forecast timing estimates when the projected actual-arm test should have enough sample to detect an effect this large, and when the future p-value would clear the selected confidence threshold if the latest observed rates persist. These are projections, not guarantees.

How spend impacts the estimates

Spend does not directly make confidence or power improve. Spend helps only if it creates more incremental unique reach.

More spend -> more new unique reach -> faster ETA

That only works if the spend is actually expanding the reached audience. If extra spend mostly shows more ads to the same people, then it increases frequency, not reach, and the ETA may not improve much.

The spend table helps the team understand whether more money is efficiently buying more unique reach by showing:

  • Avg daily spend growth
  • Avg new reach/day
  • Approx reach per $1
How conversion rates impact the estimates

Conversion rates are the other half of the story.

Reach answers: "How many people are in the test?"

Conversion rate answers: "How different are control and experiment?"

A bigger conversion-rate gap usually means the test needs less reach to become confident and powered. A tiny conversion-rate gap usually means the test needs a lot more reach.

  • Faster reach growth = shorter ETA.
  • Bigger stable conversion-rate difference = shorter ETA.
  • Smaller or noisier conversion-rate difference = longer ETA.
  • Direction opposite selected hypothesis = possibly no finite confidence ETA.

That last point is important. If the selected hypothesis is Experiment > Control, but the latest observed experiment conversion rate is below control, then confidence for Experiment > Control will not be reached if those same rates continue. More reach would only make the negative result clearer.

Team takeaway: Spend can shorten ETA estimates only when it creates more incremental unique reach. Conversion rates can shorten or lengthen the ETA because they determine how strong the observed difference is.

Step-by-step calculation details
Google Sheets formulas to embed
Use these formulas directly inside Google Sheets from your input cells. Format rate outputs as percentages where helpful. The formulas use descriptive variable names such as controlrate and experimentrate because names like p1 and p2 can be interpreted as cell references in Google Sheets.
Recommended Sheets layout: B2 = control conversions C2 = control Audience Reach D2 = experiment conversions E2 = experiment Audience Reach F2 = confidence level, e.g. 0.90 G2 = tail, one of: greater, less, two H2 = target power for statistical power panel, e.g. 0.80 I2 = control reach growth per selected period J2 = experiment reach growth per selected period; leave blank to reuse control growth K2 = p-value output L2 = observed power output M2 = projected actual-arm reach-growth periods needed N2 = confidence interval lower-bound output O2 = confidence interval upper-bound output P2 = required control N from planning helper Q2 = required experiment N from planning helper R2 = planned test length in days S2 = control unique reach per $1 T2 = experiment unique reach per $1; leave blank to reuse control reach per $1
Control conversion rate: =LET(controlconv,B2,controlreach,C2,controlconv/controlreach) Experiment conversion rate: =LET(experimentconv,D2,experimentreach,E2,experimentconv/experimentreach) Absolute difference, experiment - control: =LET(controlconv,B2,controlreach,C2,experimentconv,D2,experimentreach,E2,controlrate,controlconv/controlreach,experimentrate,experimentconv/experimentreach,experimentrate-controlrate) Relative lift: =LET(controlconv,B2,controlreach,C2,experimentconv,D2,experimentreach,E2,controlrate,controlconv/controlreach,experimentrate,experimentconv/experimentreach,IF(controlrate=0,"",(experimentrate/controlrate)-1))
P-value: =LET(controlconv,B2,controlreach,C2,experimentconv,D2,experimentreach,E2,tailchoice,LOWER(G2),controlrate,controlconv/controlreach,experimentrate,experimentconv/experimentreach,pooledrate,(controlconv+experimentconv)/(controlreach+experimentreach),standarderror,SQRT(pooledrate*(1-pooledrate)*(1/controlreach+1/experimentreach)),zscore,(experimentrate-controlrate)/standarderror,SWITCH(tailchoice,"greater",1-NORM.S.DIST(zscore,TRUE),"less",NORM.S.DIST(zscore,TRUE),"two",2*(1-NORM.S.DIST(ABS(zscore),TRUE)))) Calculated confidence, format the cell as a percent: =1-K2 False-positive risk: =K2 Z-score: =LET(controlconv,B2,controlreach,C2,experimentconv,D2,experimentreach,E2,controlrate,controlconv/controlreach,experimentrate,experimentconv/experimentreach,pooledrate,(controlconv+experimentconv)/(controlreach+experimentreach),standarderror,SQRT(pooledrate*(1-pooledrate)*(1/controlreach+1/experimentreach)),(experimentrate-controlrate)/standarderror)
Difference confidence interval lower bound: =LET(controlconv,B2,controlreach,C2,experimentconv,D2,experimentreach,E2,confidence,F2,controlrate,controlconv/controlreach,experimentrate,experimentconv/experimentreach,difference,experimentrate-controlrate,standarderror,SQRT(controlrate*(1-controlrate)/controlreach+experimentrate*(1-experimentrate)/experimentreach),zcrit,NORM.S.INV(1-(1-confidence)/2),difference-zcrit*standarderror) Difference confidence interval upper bound: =LET(controlconv,B2,controlreach,C2,experimentconv,D2,experimentreach,E2,confidence,F2,controlrate,controlconv/controlreach,experimentrate,experimentconv/experimentreach,difference,experimentrate-controlrate,standarderror,SQRT(controlrate*(1-controlrate)/controlreach+experimentrate*(1-experimentrate)/experimentreach),zcrit,NORM.S.INV(1-(1-confidence)/2),difference+zcrit*standarderror) Confidence interval crosses zero? TRUE/FALSE: =AND(N2<=0,O2>=0)
Observed power for the current effect: =LET(controlconv,B2,controlreach,C2,experimentconv,D2,experimentreach,E2,confidence,F2,tailchoice,LOWER(G2),controlrate,controlconv/controlreach,experimentrate,experimentconv/experimentreach,effectdiff,ABS(experimentrate-controlrate),unpooledse,SQRT(controlrate*(1-controlrate)/controlreach+experimentrate*(1-experimentrate)/experimentreach),effectz,effectdiff/unpooledse,zcrit,NORM.S.INV(1-IF(tailchoice="two",(1-confidence)/2,(1-confidence))),(1-NORM.S.DIST(zcrit-effectz,TRUE))+NORM.S.DIST(-zcrit-effectz,TRUE)) False-negative risk for the observed effect: =1-L2 Actual-arm power planning: Use the projected actual-arm reach-growth formulas below to estimate how many periods are needed to reach the selected power target. These formulas project control and experiment reach separately; they do not require equal arm sizes.
Projected actual-arm reach-growth periods needed: =LET(controlconv,B2,controlreach,C2,experimentconv,D2,experimentreach,E2,confidence,F2,tailchoice,LOWER(G2),targetpower,H2,controlgrowth,I2,experimentgrowth,IF(J2="",I2,J2),controlrate,controlconv/controlreach,experimentrate,experimentconv/experimentreach,zcrit,NORM.S.INV(1-IF(tailchoice="two",(1-confidence)/2,(1-confidence))),periods,SEQUENCE(50001,1,0,1),controln,controlreach+controlgrowth*periods,experimentn,experimentreach+experimentgrowth*periods,unpooledse,SQRT(controlrate*(1-controlrate)/controln+experimentrate*(1-experimentrate)/experimentn),effectz,ABS(experimentrate-controlrate)/unpooledse,projectedpower,(1-NORM.S.DIST(zcrit-effectz,TRUE))+NORM.S.DIST(-zcrit-effectz,TRUE),IFERROR(INDEX(periods,MATCH(TRUE,projectedpower>=targetpower,0)),"")) Planning-helper spend outputs: Control daily spend: =IFERROR(P2/S2/R2,"") Experiment daily spend: =IFERROR(Q2/IF(T2="",S2,T2)/R2,"") Total daily spend: =SUM(IFERROR(P2/S2/R2,0),IFERROR(Q2/IF(T2="",S2,T2)/R2,0)) Control total spend: =IFERROR(P2/S2,"") Experiment total spend: =IFERROR(Q2/IF(T2="",S2,T2),"") Total planned spend: =SUM(IFERROR(P2/S2,0),IFERROR(Q2/IF(T2="",S2,T2),0))
Method: pooled z-test for the null hypothesis that both groups have equal conversion rates. The confidence interval for the observed difference uses the unpooled standard error. For media PSA, ghost, UA, or RE tests, use unique Audience Reach as the denominator, not impression counts.
